correctHow to become a Turing Student

1. Apply to Turing
  1.1 submit your basic information
    1.1.1 full name
    1.1.2 date of birth
    1.1.3 address
    1.1.4 blood type (joke)
  1.2 provide your work history
    1.2.1 provide dates of employment
    1.2.2 provide details of job history
      1.2.2.1 provide skills learned on job
    1.2.3 provide previous education
      1.2.3.1 provide any awards given during previous education
  1.3 complete logic quiz
    1.3.1 answer eight questions
      were at least six questions answered correctly?
        yes:
        1.3.1.1 approve and move onto step 1.4
        no:
        1.3.1.1 deny and move on to *unknown step*
  1.4 interview with a Turing staff member
    1.4.1 go over work history in more detail
      1.4.1.1 provide answers to any gaps in work history
    1.4.2 ask probing questions pertaining to "why Turing? why now?"
    1.4.3 complete in person log quiz
      1.4.3.1 six questions
        1.4.3.1.1 student may use any resources immediately available to them
2. Complete Mod 0
  2.1 Complete six remote class sessions and homework
    2.1 Session 1: Expectations, Keyboard Shortcuts, Googling
      2.1.1 Expectations
        2.1.1.1 provide proper expectations for Turing environment
      2.1.2 Keyboard shortcuts
        2.1.2.1 master all 27 keyboard shorts cuts and encourage further exploration
      2.1.3 Google
        2.1.3.1 provide info on how to properly google for answers
        2.1.3.2 show good results and bad results
    2.2 Session 2: Terminal, File/folder Structure, Git Basics
      2.2.1 Terminal
        2.2.1.1 master basic terminal commands
          2.2.1.1.1 mkdir
          2.2.1.1.2 touch
          2.2.1.1.3 ls
          2.2.1.1.4 cd and cd..
          2.2.1.1.5 cat
          2.2.1.1.6 pwd
          2.2.1.1.7 rm and rm -rf
  2.2 complete Mod 0 technical assessment
    did student complete assessment?
      yes:
      2.2.1 student completed with satisfactory score
        2.2.1.1 allow student to continue on to capstone
      2.2.2 student completed but needs improvement
        2.2.2.1 advise student to correct error
          did student correct errors correctly?
            yes:
            2.2.2.1.1 allow into Turing
            no:
            2.2.2.1.1 advise student to repeat mod 0
      no:
      2.2.1 student did not complete with satisfactory score
        2.2.1.1 advise student to repeat mod 0

3. Complete the capstone
